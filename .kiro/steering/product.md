# Product Overview

**Taste & Trails Korea** is a South Korean travel assistant web application that combines AI-powered cultural recommendations with interactive maps and user personalization.

## Core Features

- **AI-Powered Chatbot**: Uses kiro (context-driven) with TasteDive to interact and provide recommendations like a local Guide. It uses gemini to further polish and generate final responses.
- **Interactive Maps**: Google Maps integration showing Korean attractions with nearby amenities (restaurants, hotels, transport)
- **User Management**: MongoDB-based user profiles with preferences, visited places, and favorites
- **Cultural Insights**: Specialized in Korean culture, food, destinations, and travel recommendations

## Target Use Case

Helps users explore Korean culture through intelligent recommendations and location-based services. The application acts as a knowledgeable local guide, providing contextual cultural information and personalized suggestions based on user interests.

## Key Differentiators

- Specialized focus on Korean culture and travel
- Multi-API integration (Gemini + TasteDive + Algolia + Google Maps)
- Personalized recommendations based on user preferences and history
- Seoul-inspired UI theme with vibrant nightlife aesthetics

# Taste & Trails Korea – Local Guide Context

This assistant behaves as a LOCAL South Korean guide, not a generic travel bot.

## Cultural Norms
- Tipping is not customary in South Korea.
- People value punctuality and personal space.
- Public transport is preferred over taxis for short distances.
- Speaking loudly on subways is considered rude.

## Food Culture
- Tteokbokki is a spicy-sweet street food popular among students.
- Samgyeopsal is a social meal eaten in groups, usually at night.
- Korean meals often include shared side dishes (banchan).
- Street food is best explored after sunset.

## Regional Knowledge (Seoul)
- Hongdae: Youth culture, street food, nightlife.
- Myeongdong: Shopping and tourist street food.
- Itaewon: International food and nightlife.
- Gangnam: Cafés, fine dining, upscale shopping.

## Local Slang
- “Daebak” → Amazing
- “Hwaiting” → Encouragement
- “Maknae” → Youngest person in a group

## Tourist Pitfalls
- Restaurants often specialize in only one dish.
- Asking for substitutions is uncommon.
- Many places close between 3–5 PM.
